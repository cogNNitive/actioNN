Paper to YouTube Script

Extract the core narrative from a mathematical epidemiology paper on zombie outbreaks and structure it into a ready-to-record YouTube script with hook, model breakdown, and call to action.

📑 Scientific PDF → 🎬 YouTube Script
⚠️ First time? Check the setup guide to install OpenCode and clone actioNN before running this sample.
1 — The input

What you'll transform

The paper "When Zombies Attack!: Mathematical Modelling of an Outbreak of Zombie Infection" by Munz, Hudea, Imad & Smith? (2009) — a mathematical epidemiology paper that applies SIR models to a zombie outbreak.

📑 munz2009a.pdf
Munz, P., Hudea, I., Imad, J. & Smith?, R. J. (2009). When Zombies Attack!: Mathematical Modelling of an Outbreak of Zombie Infection. In J. M. Tchuenche & C. Chiyaka (Eds.), Infectious Disease Modelling Research Progress (pp. 133–150). Nova Science Publishers. The paper adapts classical SIR epidemiological models to simulate a zombie outbreak. It starts with a basic Susceptible–Infected–Zombie model, then extends it with a latent period (bitten humans not yet turned), quarantine, a partial cure, and impulsive eradication events. The key finding: without aggressive, repeated attacks that kill zombies faster than they convert humans, the system tips toward total societal collapse.
📥 Download paper
2 — The workflow

Follow the steps

Run these steps in your AI agent (OpenCode, Claude Code, etc.).

Download and place the input

Download the paper PDF and place it in traNNsform/input/.

curl -o traNNsform/input/munz2009a.pdf https://pdodds.w3.uvm.edu/files/papers/others/2009/munz2009a.pdf

Run the workflow

Tell your agent to execute the paper-to-youtube workflow on the PDF.

Run the sample workflow paper-to-youtube on traNNsform/input/munz2009a.pdf. Read the paper, identify the central question and key findings, and produce a timestamped YouTube script with visual cues in traNNsform/output/. Name the file munz2009a_youtube_script.md.

Agent ingests the PDF

The agent reads the PDF, extracts the abstract, methodology, key findings, and limitations. It identifies the central narrative arc.

Agent structures the script

The agent organizes the content into a YouTube-friendly structure: hook, context, key findings, limitations, and call to action — each with estimated timestamps.

Agent adds visual cues

The agent suggests visual elements for each segment: charts to display, text overlays, b-roll concepts, and on-screen graphics to keep viewers engaged.

Review your output

Find the result at traNNsform/output/munz2009a_youtube_script.md. The agent will confirm and offer to adjust tone or level of detail.

cat traNNsform/output/munz2009a_youtube_script.md

What you'll get

# YouTube Script: When Zombies Attack! — The Math Behind the Apocalypse ## Hook (0:00–0:15) [Visual: Zombie movie montage — slow-mo horde, eerie music] "What would actually happen if zombies attacked? Mathematicians ran the numbers. Spoiler: we're doomed." ## Intro (0:15–0:45) [Visual: Host on camera + title card with "SIR Model" graphic] "Today we're breaking down a 2009 paper by Munz et al. that took classical disease modelling and applied it to the walking dead." ## The basic model (0:45–1:45) [Visual: Animated compartments — Susceptible → Infected → Zombie] "The model is a classic SIR framework, but with a twist: zombies are removed by destroying their brains (death), not by recovery. The equations show that unless zombies are eliminated faster than they convert humans, the outbreak grows without bound." ## Adding latency (1:45–2:45) [Visual: New compartment — Exposed/Latent before becoming Zombie] "The SEIR version adds a latent period: bitten humans are infected but not yet infectious — giving a small window for intervention. But even with quarantine, the model shows containment is unstable." ## Can we win? (2:45–4:00) [Visual: Graph with two curves — slow vs. aggressive response] "The only strategy that works: impulsive eradication. Regular, large-scale attacks that kill a critical number of zombies each cycle. Anything less, and the system tips toward total collapse." ## Limitations (4:00–4:30) [Visual: Text overlay — "Classical zombies only"] "The model assumes slow, classical Romero zombies. Fast zombies (28 Days Later style) would accelerate the timeline significantly." ## CTA (4:30–5:00) [Visual: Host on camera + subscribe overlay] "Could you outlast a zombie apocalypse? Check the full paper for the math behind your survival plan."
