The Rolling Stones are an English rock band formed in London in 1962. Active for over six decades, they are one of the most popular and enduring bands of the rock era. 

The classic lineup consisted of Mick Jagger on lead vocals, Keith Richards on rhythm guitar, Brian Jones on lead guitar, Bill Wyman on bass, and Charlie Watts on drums. 

In the mid-1960s, they pioneered the gritty, rhythmically driven sound that came to define hard rock. They released legendary albums like Let It Bleed and Sticky Fingers, maintaining their status as "The Greatest Rock and Roll Band in the World".
