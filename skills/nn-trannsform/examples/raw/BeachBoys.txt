The Beach Boys are an American rock band formed in Hawthorne, California, in 1961. The group's original lineup consisted of brothers Brian, Dennis, and Carl Wilson, their cousin Mike Love, and friend Al Jardine. 

Brian Wilson played bass and keyboards, Dennis Wilson played drums, Carl Wilson played lead guitar, Mike Love sang lead vocals, and Al Jardine played rhythm guitar.

They distinguished themselves with vocal harmonies and songs reflecting California youth culture of surfing, cars, and romance. Later, Brian Wilson steered the band toward complex studio productions, culminating in the masterpiece Pet Sounds.
