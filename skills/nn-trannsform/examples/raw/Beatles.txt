The Beatles were an English rock band formed in Liverpool in 1960. With a line-up comprising John Lennon, Paul McCartney, George Harrison and Ringo Starr, they are commonly regarded as the most influential band of all time. 

John Lennon played rhythm guitar and sang lead vocals. Paul McCartney played bass guitar and also sang lead. George Harrison was the lead guitarist and did backing vocals, while Ringo Starr was on the drums.

They started playing in clubs in Hamburg and Liverpool, and soon gained massive popularity, known as Beatlemania. Under the guidance of their producer George Martin, they experimented with studio sounds, creating albums like Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band. They split up in 1970.
